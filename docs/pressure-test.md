# Pressure Test / Validation Scorecard (Phase 3)

Scoring: 1 (weak) → 5 (strong). Notes included.

## 1) Real pain / urgency
**Score: 4**
- Parents/creators repeatedly cite character drift + layout as the blocker.
- “Bedtime story generators” exist, but few solve consistency well.

## 2) Market size / willingness to pay
**Score: 3.5**
- Large parent market but price sensitive.
- High willingness to pay exists for “deluxe” gifts + prosumer creators.
- Subscription can work if ongoing value (series, library, classroom packs).

## 3) Differentiation
**Score: 4**
- Consistent characters + end-to-end export is a clear wedge.
- Risk: competitors can add LoRA-ish features quickly.

## 4) Feasibility / time-to-MVP
**Score: 4**
- MVP can ship without full video: story + images + PDF + narration is straightforward.
- LoRA training pipeline adds complexity but manageable with hosted infra.

## 5) Unit economics
**Score: 3**
- Images + LoRA + TTS are manageable.
- Video is the big unknown; must be credit-gated or premium-only.

## 6) Compliance / safety risk
**Score: 3**
- Child-directed content requires strong moderation and privacy posture.
- Manageable with conservative defaults and policies.

## 7) Retention potential
**Score: 3.5**
- Retention depends on series engine + library + easy remixing.
- Educator mode could increase stickiness.

## Overall
**Weighted outcome: Strong “build”** with the recommendation to:
1) Ship MVP as illustrated book + narration (no full 4K video).
2) Make “consistent characters” the headline.
3) Add video as a premium, limited credit feature after measuring costs.

## Validation plan (next 7–10 days)
- 10 customer calls + 10 landing-page signups
- Prototype demo video of consistency across 10 pages
- Price test: $29/$59/$99 tiers with credits; deluxe per-book add-on
